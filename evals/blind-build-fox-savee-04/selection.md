# Reference Selection

I navigated the repo through the retrieval path it asks for:

1. `AGENTS.md` for the repo-wide build and validation rules.
2. `README.md`, `playbooks/poster-to-website.md`, `playbooks/world-systems.md`, `playbooks/interaction-archetypes.md`, and `playbooks/scene-kit.md` to lock the world / mechanic / page metaphor before designing.
3. `collections/music-tech.md`, `collections/culture-tech.md`, and `collections/combo-recipes.md` to keep the page in the music + culture lane instead of defaulting to generic launch-page polish.
4. `data/agent-index.json` to find the strongest adjacent references and confirm the site could be recombined from a small stack instead of copied from one source.
5. `design-md/*/README.md`, `DESIGN.md`, `meta.json`, and screenshots for the chosen references to verify the visual tone before building the page.

## Picked references

- `design-md/arte-relic` — the architectural spine. I used it for the dark archive composition, the layered poster-to-browser depth, and the feeling that the page is a collectible chamber rather than a storefront.
- `design-md/jazz-keys` — the rhythm layer. I used it for the one-stage instrument logic, the control-strip pacing, and the idea that the page should respond like a release tool instead of a dashboard.
- `design-md/design-your-own-hole` — the chroma spark. I used it to keep the release page from becoming too solemn: the yellow-blue signal contrast and tactile poster energy make the drop feel alive.

## Why this mix

- `Relic` supplies authority, depth, and the archive-room posture.
- `Jazz Keys` supplies tempo, directness, and a music-tool spine.
- `Design your own hole` supplies the bold poster-scale energy that keeps the landing page from collapsing into tasteful darkness.

## What I intentionally avoided

- A single-reference rebuild.
- SaaS templates, card grids, and testimonial-led landing-page grammar.
- Recreating a poster slab instead of translating the source logic into a browser-native stage.
