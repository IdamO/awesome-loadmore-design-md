# Reference Selection

I navigated the repo the way the agent guide intended:

1. `AGENTS.md` for the retrieval strategy.
2. `data/agent-index.json` and the starter-pack docs in `collections/`.
3. Specific `design-md/*/DESIGN.md` + screenshots to verify the visual tone before recombining.

## Picked references

- `design-md/design-your-own-hole` — the chroma engine. I pulled its electric blue / acid yellow contrast and the tactile, toy-like confidence because it gives the page a loud Gen-Z pop backbone instead of generic launch-page polish.
- `design-md/jazz-keys` — the rhythm layer. I used it for the dark sonic pacing, stripped-down UI logic, and the feeling that the page should read like a track list, not a dashboard.
- `design-md/harley-weir` — the editorial authority. I used its white-space discipline, serif-led posture, and fashion-world framing so the page can span music, fashion, and culture without losing taste.
- `design-md/smc-real-time` — the archive / room structure. I used it to keep the information architecture modular and exhibition-like, so the landing page feels like a scene you enter rather than a sheet of marketing copy.

## Why this mix

- `Design your own hole` supplies the bold color tension.
- `Jazz Keys` supplies tempo and restraint.
- `Harley Weir` keeps the page from collapsing into pure pop noise.
- `SMC REAL:TIME` keeps the structure legible and culture-tech adjacent.

## What I intentionally avoided

- Pure SaaS refs that would flatten the tone into product dashboard energy.
- A single-site imitation. The final page is a recombination of one chroma source, one rhythm source, one editorial source, and one archive source.
