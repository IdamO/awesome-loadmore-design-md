# Type Systems

Good digital taste comes from type role contrast, not just a cool headline font.

## Four-role stack

Every public-facing page should think in these roles:

1. Authority — display serif or elevated grotesk that sets the emotional posture
2. Workhorse — primary reading/interface sans
3. Freak — high-personality accent face used at peaks only
4. Utility — mono, bitmap, or technical face for metadata and controls

## Foundry directions

### Authority families

- Dinamo / Monument Grotesk family zone
- Klim / Tiempos or Founders Grotesk zone
- Pangram Pangram serif-display hybrids
- OH no Type when you need a louder editorial voice

### Workhorse families

- sturdy grotesks with multiple weights and good italics
- neutral enough to carry dense UI, but not invisible

### Freak families

- keep one family reserved for peaks: campaign words, drop moments, major stats, mode labels
- do not use it for all headings or it stops feeling special

### Utility faces

- mono, bitmap, low-fi terminal, or technical face for timestamps, system copy, controls, and archive labels

## Good pairings by world

### Club Instrument
- Authority: elegant serif or high-drama display
- Workhorse: technical grotesk
- Utility: mono or digital display
- Freak: use sparingly for launch moments

### Luxury Archive
- Authority: serious serif
- Workhorse: calm sans
- Utility: mono for labels and object numbers
- Freak: optional, usually unnecessary

### Collage Core
- Authority: disciplined base grotesk or serif
- Workhorse: readable sans
- Utility: xerox-ish mono or bitmap
- Freak: strong display for torn-poster peaks only

### Cozy Desktop
- Authority: humane sans or soft serif
- Workhorse: desktop-friendly sans
- Utility: bitmap or mono
- Freak: optional playful accent

### Playable Poster
- Authority: loud display face
- Workhorse: stable sans
- Utility: condensed mono or badge face
- Freak: often the same as the authority, but rationed carefully

## Type rules

- Use the authority face for voice, not for every sentence
- Let the workhorse do most of the labor
- Keep metadata visually distinct with a utility face
- Maintain at least one hard contrast: serif vs sans, soft vs hard, calm vs loud
- If every line is expressive, nothing is expressive
